  • I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics and Operations Research at UNC Chapel Hill under Prof. Vladas Pipiras.

üîç Research interests

  • How to utilize data efficiently when multiple simulation codes for the same quantity with different fidelities are available?
  • We develop multi-fidelity strategies for estimating the distribution of high-fidelity outputs, with a particular focus on extreme events. We explore two strategies for leveraging low-fidelity data:
  • (i) Selective sampling: Low-fidelity model outputs are first evaluated to guide the sampling of high-fidelity outputs. We apply this in a non-parametric setting, incorporating extreme value theory to improve tail estimation.
  • (ii) Data fusion: A larger amount of offline, independently obtained low-fidelity data is used to construct estimators with reduced variance. We first develop this approach in a parametric setting, then extend it to a non-parametric setting.
