Hi, I'm Minji Kim!

I am pursuing a Ph.D. in Statistics and Operations Research at UNC Chapel Hill under the guidance of Prof. Vladas Pipiras. My research focuses on leveraging multiple computer models with varying levels of fidelity (in terms of accuracy and cost) to achieve more accurate estimations, a field known as multi-fidelity (MF) modeling. Our works in this field incorporate semi-supervised learning problems that address partially labeled data.

I am also exploring data-driven approaches to solving PDE problems, with current work on reduced-order modeling and PINNs with implicit neural representations. This project builds on connections from my internship with the LLNL team in summer 2022. I also interned at Moloco in summer 2023. More details are in the Research tab, and you can also view my Resume.
